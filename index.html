How Notsdotq Works: A Quick Guide

Core Concept: Parent → Child

The Notsdotq ecosystem is built on a simple but powerful idea: tokens can be "parents" or "children."

  • To get a child token, you burn its parent.
  • You can also claim back the parent by returning the child (1-for-1).

V3 vs. V4 Tokens: The Multiplier

There are two main types of tokens in this ecosystem:

  • V3 Tokens: Have a fixed multiplier (usually 1x). This means you burn 1 parent to get 1 child. Simple and stable.
  • V4 Tokens: Have a dynamic multiplier. Each time the total supply is minted, the cost to mint goes up. This makes them deflationary by design.

What's a Spine?

A "spine" is just a chain of tokens. For example: Token A is the parent of Token B, and Token B is the parent of Token C. Spines can be short or long, and they're what create deep, interconnected token relationships.

The Big Question: Mint or Buy?

Because of how multipliers work, you can sometimes get a token for cheaper by minting it instead of buying it on the open market (and vice-versa). SpineTracker is the essential tool for this.
